White Lasagna

Lasagna is a traditional Italian main dish consisting of alternating layers of long, flat pasta noodles, tomato or cream sauce, and cheese (typically, mozzarella, parmesan, or ricotta, or a combination of all three).
Lasagna may also include layers of sautéed vegetables or ground meat, like beef or lamb. The pasta is boiled before assembly, and then the entire dish is cooked further in the oven.

Ingredients
For the béchamel:
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 Yellow onion
  • check_box_outline_blank 2 Whole cloves
  • check_box_outline_blank 2 Bay leaves
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 Quart whole milk
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 Stick unsalted butter
  • check_box_outline_blank ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • check_box_outline_blank ¼ teaspoon freshly ground nutmeg
  • check_box_outline_blank Kosher salt, to taste
  • check_box_outline_blank Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
For the filling:
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 Pound italian sausage
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 15-ounce container ricotta cheese
  • check_box_outline_blank ½ cup chopped fresh parsley
  • check_box_outline_blank ½ cup chopped fresh basil
  • check_box_outline_blank 2 tablespoons chopped fresh or dried oregano
  • check_box_outline_blank 3 garlic cloves, grated
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 pound mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • check_box_outline_blank 1 package fresh or no-boil lasagna noodles
  • 1
    Slice the onion in half.
  • 2
    Use one whole clove to attach a bay leaf to each onion half by piercing through the bay leaf and the first layer of onion with the pointed end of the clove.
  • 3
    Place the onion halves and milk in a large saucepan and warm over low heat, taking care not to boil or scald the milk.
  • 4
    Make a roux by melting the butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  • 5
    Whisk in the flour and cook the roux until it is lightly golden but not brown, about 2–3 minutes.
  • 6
    Remove the onion from the milk and gradually whisk the warmed milk into the roux.
  • 7
    Stir in the fresh nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  • 8
    Reduce the heat to medium and allow the béchamel sauce to thicken, about 5 minutes.
  • 9
    Reduce the heat to its lowest setting and cover the sauce while you prepare the remaining ingredients.
  • 10
    In a large skillet over medium-high heat, crumble the sausage and cook until browned, about 8 minutes.
  • 11
    Strain excess fat and liquid from the cooked sausage crumbles and set aside.
  • 12
    Place the ricotta cheese in a small bowl and add the grated Parmesan, chopped parsley, basil, oregano, and grated garlic. Stir to combine, then season to taste with salt and pepper.
  • 13
    Preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • 14
    Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with a tablespoon of olive oil.
  • 15
    Ladle about ½ cup of the béchamel sauce onto the bottom of the baking dish.
  • 16
    Top the sauce with a single layer of noodles, followed by half of the ricotta cheese mixture, half of the sausage, and one-third of the shredded cheese, followed by a ¼ cup of the béchamel.
  • 17
    Add another layer of noodles, then top with ½ cup of the béchamel, the remaining ricotta mixture, the remaining sausage, and another third of the shredded cheese.
  • 18
    Finish the lasagna with a top layer of noodles. Pour the remaining sauce in an even layer over the top and sprinkle with the rest of the shredded cheese.
  • 19
    Cover the lasagna with aluminum foil and bake until bubbling, about 30 minutes.
  • 20
    Remove the foil and bake until the top layer of béchamel and mozzarella is bubbling and crispy and golden brown on the edges, about 15 more minutes.
  • 21
    Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.
